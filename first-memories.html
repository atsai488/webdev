Starting college was a weird time in my life. I knew it would shift who I was dramatically, so I wanted to document as much of the start as possible. I wanted to mark every moment where I met someone new, so I could look back in 10 years and laugh about how I first connected with a lifelong friend. A random trend I noticed was that no matter what I would always find myself on the ground because I was being overdramatic. Whether it was because I just had enough from my classes or because I was dealing with silly little things in my life. I would just collapse on the floor and my friends would laugh at me. It became a staple of my identity, and my friend even designated a part of the floor of her room to me.
For one reason or another, my first semester was tumultuous. I cringe at a lot of the memories I can recall and would never put myself in a situation like that now. However, that does not mean I regret any of the memories I have made. I think my first semester was a time where I grew the most in my life as a person, and I am thankful for the people who helped me through that process. In the end, everything worked out and I ended my first semester on a high note as I ice skated next to the Crypto arena.
Austin Tsai's Freshman Reflection of College
