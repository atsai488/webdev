Before I began college, I was stressed. I was leaving my friends whom I had known since kindergarten. For the first time in my life, I would travel to a foreign land independently, and I was in charge of who I was around. After my first year of college, I confidently declare I have met quite the group of people. The people I am friends with now are not like those I surrounded myself with back home, but that is not bad. Initially, I felt like I was trying to search for what I lost, and the process was tumultuous. I spent nights, weeks, and months comparing the people I met to people back home and it always felt wrong. I tried molding new people into old identities, and I felt fake. Only after I gave up on this search did I truly accept people for who they were. It shook things up in my life, but that’s what college is all about — Change.
The people I am around now are not the same as I began with, but that is okay with me. I feel like I have settled into a place where I can be myself without thinking twice about my actions and it feels quite liberating. I still have not regained what I have lost, but I think the change is what makes the college experience worth it. I am not the same person I was, and it is not a bad thing.
Austin Tsai's Freshman Reflection of College
